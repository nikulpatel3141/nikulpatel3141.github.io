Participants in the UK financial market are required to disclose their positions when above a certain threshold (0.2% of shares outstanding as of writing) — these are published daily by the FCA.

## Code

The code to generate this report is on [GitHub](https://github.com/nikulpatel3141/UK-Short-Tracker), along with a README explaining how it works.

## Metrics

- **Return** — the (adjusted) return of the security
- **PnL** — the disclosed PnL from the short position
- **Days to Cover** — the number of days to fully liquidate the position at 100% trading volume (22-day average)

### Context

- A lookback period of 5 days is used since funds disclosing shorts typically won't be actively trading in and out of positions due to slippage concerns
- Days to Cover is a useful metric: if large and shorts perform badly, it's difficult to exit the position
- Many of these funds are long-short, meaning PnL relative to a benchmark (e.g. VUKE) may be more useful than raw PnL
